02 / 07
PRIORITY ACTION

YOUR 48-HOUR EMERGENCY CHECKLIST

EXECUTE IN ORDER. BEFORE YOU DO ANYTHING ELSE.

▶ HOUR 01–04 CRITICAL
  • Breathe. Literally. This is a plot twist, not the end. You have time to think clearly before you act.
  • Save all personal files from your work device — access can be revoked within hours. Personal docs, contacts, anything that belongs to you.
  • Write down your manager's name, HR contact, separation date, and the exact language used. You'll need this for unemployment filings and possibly legal action.
  • Note the exact date and time of the layoff conversation. This is your base date for unemployment claims and COBRA deadlines.
  • Confirm health insurance status: does coverage end today or end of month? Call HR. This sets your COBRA election window.
  • 🚨 DO NOT post on social media. Not even a vague "big changes ahead." Not today. Not tonight.
▶ HOUR 04–24 IMPORTANT
  • File for unemployment insurance — file the same day or next business day. UI does not back-pay before your filing date. Every day delayed = money lost.  usa.gov/unemployment-benefits  ·  DC: does.dc.gov/page/file-claim
  • Review your severance — do NOT sign anything for at least 24 hours. Under ADEA, you have 21 days to review (45 if part of a group layoff). Source: eeoc.gov
  • If you received COBRA paperwork, note your deadline: 60 days to elect. Set a calendar reminder right now — missing this is permanent.
  • Call your bank. Ask about hardship deferral or payment reduction programs. Most lenders won't advertise them. You have to ask.
  • Tell one trusted person — someone who won't panic, gossip, or immediately launch into job search advice. Just a witness to the moment.
▶ HOUR 24–48 STABILIZE
  • Calculate your financial runway: savings ÷ monthly_expenses = months_of_cushion. Write the number down. Knowing it is better than fearing it.
  • Sort bills: what can be deferred (student loans, utilities — call and ask) vs. what cannot (rent, car, health insurance).
  • Check SNAP eligibility. In DC: no asset test, income limit 200% FPL. Average benefit $200–$400/month. Apply: dhs.dc.gov
  • Establish a minimal daily structure. Wake time + one job-task block + a cut-off hour. Structure fights depression. Source: APA research on routine and mental health.
  • Change passwords on all personal accounts — especially anything shared with work devices, SSO, or VPNs. Your digital security is now yours alone.
SOURCES: DOL.GOV — UNEMPLOYMENT INSURANCE · DC DOES — FILE A CLAIM · DC DHS — SNAP · EEOC — ADEA SEVERANCE RIGHTS
03 / 07
HR INTELLIGENCE

THE 5 THINGS NOT TO SAY OR DO

GROUNDED IN HR EXPERIENCE. FROM SOMEONE WHO'S BEEN ON BOTH SIDES OF THAT TABLE.

// ERROR_01
POST ON LINKEDIN WITHIN THE FIRST 24 HOURS
Why it destroys you: Emotional posts permanently shape how hiring managers perceive you. They look back. A calm, considered post in week two outperforms a raw one posted in hour two every time. You don't owe the internet your grief.
// ERROR_02
SAY ANYTHING NEGATIVE ABOUT YOUR EMPLOYER
Why it destroys you: Reference checks are real. Former colleagues talk. Anything posted, emailed, or said professionally can follow you into your next interview. Vent to a therapist or trusted friend. Not Slack. Not Twitter. Not a "private" work group.
// ERROR_03
SIGN YOUR SEVERANCE AGREEMENT IMMEDIATELY
Why it destroys you: Under ADEA, you have 21 days to review — 45 days if part of a group layoff. A one-time payment in exchange for waiving your legal rights deserves at least one night of sleep. Get it reviewed. Source: eeoc.gov
// ERROR_04
SKIP UNEMPLOYMENT BECAUSE YOU EXPECT TO FIND WORK FAST
Why it destroys you: Median job search for experienced workers: 5–6 months. UI replaces 40–50% of prior wages. You paid into this system with every paycheck. Using it is not a moral failing — it's what it's there for. Source: BLS Job Search Duration Data
// ERROR_05
ISOLATE
Why it destroys you: Job loss ranks #8 of 43 on the Holmes-Rahe Stress Inventory. Isolation amplifies anxiety, depression, and bad decision-making. Tell one person today. The data is clear: connection is a buffer. Source: Holmes & Rahe, Journal of Psychosomatic Research (1967)
SOURCES: EEOC — ADEA SEVERANCE REVIEW RIGHTS · BLS — JOB SEARCH DURATION · HOLMES & RAHE STRESS SCALE, JOURNAL OF PSYCHOSOMATIC RESEARCH VOL.11 (1967)

HIDDEN UNLOCK

THE BLACK BOX KEY

MOST PEOPLE IN THE DMV NEVER LEARN THIS EXISTS. IT CAN TRIPLE-STACK YOUR SURVIVAL FUNDING.

The Department of Rehabilitative Services (called DORS, RSA, or DARS depending on your state) provides vocational rehabilitation funding — job training, certifications, college coursework, assistive technology, transportation, and more. It's not just for people with physical disabilities. Neurodivergent individuals, people with anxiety/depression, chronic illness, ADHD, PTSD, learning differences — all may qualify. The threshold is whether your condition creates a barrier to employment. Not whether it's severe.

// THE TRIPLE STACK — LAYER YOUR BENEFITS

1
Unemployment Insurance (UI) — ~40–50% of prior wages while you search
STACKED ON TOP
2
DORS / RSA / DARS Vocational Rehab — training, certs, tuition, tools, transport paid for you
STACKED ON TOP
3
Local Emergency Assistance — SNAP, food pantries, LIHEAP, rental assistance simultaneously
ALL THREE ARE LEGAL TO RECEIVE SIMULTANEOUSLY. NONE DISQUALIFIES THE OTHERS.

FUNDING UNLOCKS

WORKFORCE PROGRAMS: MONEY YOU'RE LEAVING BEHIND

Beyond unemployment and SNAP, there are federally-funded workforce programs that can pay for your retraining, certifications, and job search — directly. Most people never apply because they don't know they exist.

KEY PROGRAM

WIOA — WORKFORCE INNOVATION AND OPPORTUNITY ACT

WHAT IT IS Federal program providing career counseling, job training vouchers (ITAs — Individual Training Accounts), resume help, job placement, and supportive services to adults and dislocated workers.
WHO QUALIFIES Dislocated Worker track: If you lost your job through no fault of your own (layoff, company closure, federal RIF) — you qualify regardless of income. Adult track: 18+ with barriers to employment.
WHAT IT PAYS FOR Approved training programs, certifications, community college, apprenticeships, on-the-job training (OJT), work readiness, supportive services (transport, childcare), paid work experience.
DC dcworks.dc.gov — WIOA Training Services  ·  DC American Job Centers (appointment required)
MARYLAND labor.maryland.gov/employment/wioa.shtml  ·  mdworks.com — Maryland Workforce Exchange
VIRGINIA virginiacareerworks.com — Rapid Response  ·  DOL WIOA Adult Program
HOW TO ACCESS WALK INTO OR CALL YOUR LOCAL AMERICAN JOB CENTER. ASK FOR A WIOA DISLOCATED WORKER ASSESSMENT. FREE, NO COST.
TRADE DISPLACED

TAA — TRADE ADJUSTMENT ASSISTANCE

WHAT IT IS Federal program for workers who lost jobs due to increased imports, outsourcing, or shifts in trade policy. Provides training, income support (TRA — Trade Readjustment Allowances), job search, and relocation allowances.
WHO QUALIFIES Workers whose jobs moved offshore, were eliminated due to foreign competition, or whose employer shifted production to a country with which the US has a trade agreement. A petition of 3+ workers must be filed — your union rep, HR, or you + coworkers can file together.
BENEFITS Up to 130 weeks of income support (TRA) during approved training · Full tuition reimbursement · Job search allowance up to $1,500 · Relocation allowance up to 90% of moving costs · HCTC (Health Coverage Tax Credit) for COBRA costs
DC does.dc.gov/service/trade-adjustment-assistance
MARYLAND labor.maryland.gov/employment/taa.shtml
FEDERAL FILING dol.gov/agencies/eta/tradeact  ·  Email: taa.petition@dol.gov  ·  Phone: 1-888-365-6822
NEW 2026

WORKFORCE PELL GRANT

WHAT IT IS Starting July 1, 2026, Pell Grants can fund short-term training programs (8–15 weeks, 150–599 hours). IT, healthcare, skilled trades, early childhood ed. Even if you already have a bachelor's degree.
HOW TO APPLY File FAFSA at studentaid.gov. Income from unemployment counts — apply regardless. Award is based on financial need.
NOTE Stackable with WIOA ITAs. Double-fund your retraining.
